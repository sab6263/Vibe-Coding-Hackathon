Featured Project Publication

Designing Trustworthy AI Support for Emergency Dispatch Centers

A Human-Centered, Context-Aware AI Concept for High-Stress Decision Making

Authors

Sarah Baumann
in collaboration with Giacomo Britz & Lukas Kolb

Institution / Customer

University Project in collaboration with Emergency Dispatch Centers (Fire & EMS) in Bavaria, Germany

Download PDF Report

Problem Statement

Emergency dispatchers operate in high-stress, time-critical environments. They must simultaneously communicate with distressed callers, document information in rigid legacy systems, and make legally critical decisions under immense pressure.

While AI is often proposed for efficiency, dispatchers deeply distrust automation that is opaque or intrusive. The core challenge: How can AI support dispatchers without undermining trust, responsibility, or human expertise?

Hover to switch between Research and UI Concept

Method

We conducted in-depth interviews with professional dispatchers in Integrated Dispatch Centers (Munich & Ingolstadt), mapping workflows and identifying cognitive overload points where AI could provide non-intrusive support.

Analysis of legacy software and multi-screen environments showed that poorly designed AI risks becoming a burden. We focused on mapping "call intake to disposition" to find the right moments for assistance.

We synthesized insights around explainability, human-in-the-loop control, and emotional safety, ensuring the dispatcher remains the ultimate authority in every high-stakes situation.

Designing an AI assistant as an embedded support layer that favors contextual reasoning over rigid rules, supporting human intuition ("Bauchgefühl") rather than attempting to replace it.

Outcomes

Assistive Partner Model

AI suggestions are optional and fully reversible. No "black box" decisions; the human dispatcher always makes the final call.

Explainable Recommendations

Every recommendation includes a short, readable rationale and visible alternatives to calibrate trust appropriately.

Context-Aware Intelligence

AI accounts for resource recovery times, traffic, geography, and even the emotional state of the caller to provide better suggestions.

Cognitive Load Reduction

The system is seamlessly embedded into existing workflows—no extra monitors or fragmented windows for the dispatcher.

We successfully developed a fully functional prototype of this AI-supported system, demonstrating how explainable UI can build long-term trust in high-stakes environments.
